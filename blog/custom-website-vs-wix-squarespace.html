Custom Website vs Wix / Squarespace: Which Actually Pays Off?

Updated Mar 20267 min read

Every business owner hits this fork: drag-and-drop on Wix or Squarespace, or pay a developer for a custom build? Both are valid. The mistake is choosing based on price alone instead of what the site needs to do.

How they really compare

FactorWix / SquarespaceCustom build
Upfront costLow (₱0–₱3k/mo)Higher (₱15k+)
Time to launchDaysWeeks
Unique brandingTemplate-ishFully yours
Custom featuresLimitedAnything
Speed & SEO controlConstrainedFull
Ownership / lock-inTrappedYou own it
Ongoing effortYou maintainDev or you

When a builder is the right call

  • You need a simple presence fast and cheap.
  • The site is mostly informational (hours, menu, portfolio).
  • You are comfortable maintaining it yourself.
  • You don't need custom functionality or deep SEO.

When a custom build pays off

  • The site must generate leads or sales — conversion-focused design matters.
  • You need bespoke features (booking, members, integrations, dashboards).
  • Brand differentiation is part of the strategy.
  • You want full control over speed, accessibility, and search performance.
  • You plan to scale and don't want to outgrow a platform.
A builder gets you online. A custom site gets you ahead — but only if the business actually needs the edge.

The hidden cost of "free"

Builders lock your content and design inside their platform. Moving later means rebuilding from scratch. If there is any chance you'll outgrow it, a custom site avoids that rebuild cost — and you fully own the asset.

My honest recommendation

If the website is a business tool meant to bring in money, invest in custom. If it is a digital business card, a builder is fine. Not sure which you are? That question alone is worth a short call.

Frequently asked questions

Is Wix or Squarespace good enough for a business?
For a simple brochure or hobby site, yes — they are fast and cheap to start. They get limiting when you need custom functionality, unique branding, or strong organic search performance.
Why is a custom site more expensive?
Because you are paying for strategy, a design built only for your brand, clean code, and flexibility to grow. You own it fully and are not locked to one platform's pricing or limits.
Can I move my site off a builder later?
It is possible but rarely clean — your content and design are trapped in the platform. Migrating means rebuilding. If you might outgrow a builder, a custom site avoids that cost.
