Freelance Web Developer vs Agency in the Philippines: Which to Hire?

Updated Mar 20266 min read

The "freelancer or agency?" question isn't about which is better — it's about which fits your project, budget, and risk tolerance. Both can deliver a great site. Here is how to decide without regret.

Side-by-side

FactorFreelancerAgency
CostLower (less overhead)Higher
Direct communicationYou talk to the doerOften via PM
Team / skill breadthOne personDesign + dev + copy
Backup if unavailableSingle pointTeam cover
Speed on big scopeLimited bandwidthParallel work
Ongoing marketingVariesUsually offered

Pick a freelancer when…

  • You have one focused website (brochure, business, or a single store).
  • Budget matters and you want more build per peso.
  • You value talking directly to the person doing the work.
  • The scope is clear and doesn't need a full team.

Pick an agency when…

  • You need a team: design, development, copy, and project management.
  • Deadlines are tight and you need parallel bandwidth + backup cover.
  • The website is part of a bigger marketing retainer.
  • You want one vendor accountable for strategy, not just code.
A strong freelancer beats a weak agency every time. Compare the actual person or team, portfolios, and what's included — not the label.

The real risks (and how to reduce them)

Freelancer risk: single-point dependency. If they go quiet, you stall. Protect yourself with a written contract, milestone payments, and a clause that source code is handed over on payment.

Agency risk: cost and weaker personal attention; your project can sit behind bigger clients. Protect yourself by agreeing on response times and a named point of contact up front.

Questions to ask either way

  1. Can I see 2–3 sites you built end-to-end?
  2. What exactly is included (copy, images, revisions, fixes)?
  3. How do we communicate and how fast is feedback?
  4. Who owns the code, and when do I get it?
  5. What happens after launch — support, training, updates?

Frequently asked questions

Is a freelancer cheaper than an agency?
Usually, yes — you skip account management and overhead, so more of your budget goes to the build. But a very cheap freelancer may lack design or strategy depth, so compare the actual deliverable, not just the rate.
When should I pick an agency?
Choose an agency when you need a team (designer + developer + copywriter + PM), tight deadlines with backup coverage, or ongoing marketing beyond the site. For a focused single-site project, a strong freelancer is often the better fit.
What is the biggest risk with a freelancer?
Single-point dependency: if they go silent or get busy, progress stalls. Reduce it with a clear contract, milestones, and source-code handover terms from the start.
