How Long Does It Take to Build a Website? A Realistic Timeline

Updated Mar 20266 min read

"Two weeks" is a real answer for a simple site — and so is "three months" for a custom platform. The range exists because timeline depends almost entirely on readiness and scope, not on how fast someone can type code. Here is what a normal build actually looks like.

Typical timelines by type

ProjectRealistic window
Brochure / landing (content ready)1–2 weeks
Business site (5–12 pages)3–6 weeks
E-commerce5–10 weeks
Custom web app2–4+ months

The phases (and where time goes)

  1. Discovery & strategy (2–5 days). Goals, audience, sitemap, references. Skipping this is the #1 cause of rework.
  2. Design (3–10 days). Layout, visual direction, desktop + mobile. Faster when you give clear references.
  3. Build (1–3 weeks). Development, CMS, integrations. This is the part developers are fast at.
  4. Content & polish (ongoing). Copy, images, SEO, speed. Often the longest stretch.
  5. Review & launch (3–7 days). Feedback rounds, QA, domain/hosting, go-live.

What actually causes delays

Nearly every slipped deadline comes from the same places:

  • Missing content. "I'll write the copy later" is the classic timeline-killer. Build and content in parallel, or finish copy first.
  • Slow feedback. A 5-day wait between review rounds adds weeks.
  • Scope creep. "While you're at it, can we also add a blog, a portal, and bookings?" — each adds real time.
  • Missing access. Hosting, domain, social, payment accounts not handed over.
The coding is rarely the bottleneck. The bottleneck is almost always waiting on words, pictures, and decisions.

How to ship faster

  • Write copy and gather images before the build starts.
  • Share 2–3 reference sites you like so design direction is clear.
  • Agree on a fixed number of revision rounds up front.
  • Hand over all logins (domain, hosting, payment) on day one.
  • Book a standing 48-hour feedback window for each review.

Frequently asked questions

How fast can a simple website be ready?
With content ready, a 1–5 page brochure site can launch in 1–2 weeks. The build itself is fast; waiting on copy and images is what usually drags it out.
What causes most website delays?
Missing content (copy, photos, login access), slow feedback, and mid-build scope changes. These account for the vast majority of slipped timelines, not the coding.
Can I rush a website?
Yes, for a simple site, if you provide all content up front and approve quickly. Complex e-commerce or custom features cannot be rushed without hurting quality or security.
